## Users (in priority order)

1. **The fall homeowner.** Age 35–65. Owns a Pacific Northwest single-family home built 1960–2010. Just noticed water spilling over the gutter edge during the first October downpour. Will get 3 quotes before deciding. Wants a fair price, no pressure, real workmanship. *Mostly on mobile, often researching at night.*
2. **The replace-it-now homeowner.** Age 50–75. Has lived in the home 15+ years, gutters are clearly failing (sagging, leaking, fascia rot). Senior or veteran discount matters. Comfortable on phone but appreciates a clean website. *Frequently on tablet or desktop.*
3. **The proactive maintainer.** Age 40–60. Just bought the home or had a moss-covered roof issue. Researching gutter guards in particular. Comparison-shops on price/feature. Reads reviews carefully. *Usually desktop, often a 2nd monitor at work.*
4. **The contractor / property manager.** Age 30–55. Booking work for multiple properties. Wants speed, accurate quotes, and willingness to do volume.

## Brand voice

**Honest tradesman. Quiet authority. Pacific Northwest local.**

- Speak like a craftsman who actually gets on the ladder, not a salesperson.
- Concrete numbers and specifics over puffery (lengths, prices, timelines, materials).
- Acknowledge that this is rain country. Use weather as setting, not gimmick.
- Confidence without bravado. Never "BEST IN SEATTLE!!!" — that reads as a national chain pretending to be local.
- Plainspoken. Cut adjectives. Use periods.

**Sentence test:** Could a 60-year-old Bothell homeowner read it out loud without rolling her eyes? If no, rewrite.

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Same-week estimates from Marysville to Olympia."
- ✅ "Most homes finish in 4–6 hours. We treat your yard like our own."
- ✅ "Cedar fascia rots behind your gutters. We replace it before re-hanging."
- ❌ "Best-in-class, premium, world-renowned gutter solutions delivered with passion."
- ❌ "Revolutionary gutter technology designed for the modern Pacific Northwest homeowner!"

## Anti-references

What this brand is **not**, and visual / verbal references we explicitly avoid.

- **Not a national franchise.** No safety-orange, no chevrons, no stock-photo guys-in-hardhats fist-bumping.
- **Not a gradient SaaS template.** No purple-to-blue hero gradients, no glassmorphism cards, no "AI-powered" copy.
- **Not minimalist startup whitespace.** Too cold for a 60-year-old homeowner deciding on a $4,000 install.
- **Not a "we're just like family!" home-services cliché site.** No script fonts, no rotating-checkmark stock graphics.
- **Not LeafFilter / national gutter chains.** Their aesthetic is a yellow-and-black warning sign. We are the local alternative — pacific, considered, craft-trade.

## Strategic principles

These guide every design and content decision, in priority order.

1. **Trust at first scroll.** A new visitor in 3 seconds knows: real local company, 30+ years, 5-star rated, easy to contact. If those four don't land before the fold ends, the design has failed.
2. **One canonical contact path.** One phone number, one email, one address — used identically everywhere on the site, GMB, and citations. NAP consistency is non-negotiable.
3. **Honest pricing posture.** "Free written estimate, valid for 12 months" appears near every CTA. We're the value brand — own it, don't hide it.
4. **Geography is the moat.** Each city we serve gets a real, unique landing page with local context, local photos, local reviews. Generic city pages are forbidden.
5. **Mobile is the primary surface.** ~70% of fall homeowner traffic is mobile. Every component is designed mobile-first, sticky-CTA always visible, tap targets ≥ 44px.
6. **Conversion paths are plural.** Not everyone calls. Surface call, text, online estimate, and instant-range estimator on every key page.
7. **Editorial weight beats template polish.** This site reads like a Pacific Northwest publication's profile of a craft business, not a Wix template for "Plumbing Services."

## Anti-patterns (will be rejected on review)

- "30+ years" stated in one section, "40 years combined" in another. Pick one and never deviate.
- Multiple phone numbers anywhere. There is one number.
- Stock alt text from a different state ("Yulee, FL" was real, never again).
- Footer links pointing to other companies' policy pages.
- City pages that swap a city name and reuse identical body copy.
- Hero copy that doesn't immediately say what we sell + where we operate.
- Modal as the first conversion ask. Inline forms first; modal only for the "request a real estimate" deep-funnel step.
- Auto-playing audio. Auto-playing video over 3 MB. Carousels on desktop without arrows + keyboard nav.
- Any decorative blur, gradient text, or hero-metric-template SaaS cliché.
